Current Life Expectancy Trends

  • Average Lifespan : As of 2025, the average life expectancy in Malaysia is approximately 76 years.
  • Healthy Life Expectancy : Only 67 years, meaning most people spend nearly 9 years in poor health or with a reduced quality of life.
  • Long-Term Trend : Increased from 54 years in the 1950s to the current 75 years, with projections reaching 81 years by 2050.
  • Rise in Centenarians : From fewer than 10 individuals in the early 1970s to over 1,000 today.

⚠️Challenges Ahead in Malaysia

1. Inadequate Retirement Security:
  • Only 36% of EPF (Employees Provident Fund) members aged 55 have sufficient savings (around RM240,000) to sustain a retirement income of RM1,000 per month for 20 years.
  • About 40% of workers are in the gig economy or non-traditional employment, most of whom lack retirement coverage.
2. Accelerating Population Aging:
  • The population aged 65 and above has reached 7.3% and is projected to rise to 15% by 2050, officially classifying Malaysia as an "aged nation."
  • The fertility rate has dropped to 1.7, shrinking the future working-age population and increasing dependency on fewer contributors.
3. Pressure on Healthcare & Social Support Systems:
  • The widening gap between healthy life expectancy and actual lifespan calls for stronger public health measures, preventive care, and community support.
  • Despite increased government healthcare funding (RM45.3 billion), more investment is needed to meet long-term care demands.
4.Growing Fiscal Burden:
  • Pension expenditures are expected to reach RM223 billion by 2030.
  • Rising social security costs for the elderly require continued budget expansion and policy reforms to sustain support systems.
